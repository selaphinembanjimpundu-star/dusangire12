BUSINESS MODEL CANVAS - Hospital E-Commerce Restaurant

1. Customer Segments
Primary Customers (Inside Hospital):
- Hospital patients (inpatient and outpatient)
- Doctors, nurses, technicians, and staff
- Caregivers and visitors
- Long-term patients with ongoing dietary needs

Secondary Customers (Outside Hospital):
- Nearby residents
- Workers in the area
- Students or others seeking healthy, affordable meals
- Health-conscious customers (fitness and diet plans)

2. Value Propositions
For Patients:
- Healthy, medically-approved meals for quicker recovery
- Diet-specific options (diabetic, low-sodium, post-surgery, high-protein)
- Convenient bedside ordering through an app
- Fast delivery within the hospital

For Medical Staff:
- Around-the-clock access to quick, healthy meals
- No need to leave the workplace to find food
- Subscription meal plans for busy shifts

For Outside Customers:
- High-quality healthy meals at reasonable prices
- Convenient e-commerce ordering and home or office delivery
- Access to medically-recommended meal options

General Value:
- Technology-enabled restaurant
- Clean, modern, fast, and reliable service
- Meal plans created by nutritionists
- Clear ingredient lists and health-focused recipes

3. Channels
- E-commerce website
- Mobile app (Android and iOS)
- On-site restaurant outlet within the hospital
- Delivery within the hospital (ward delivery)
- Home and office delivery (for outside customers)
- Social media (Facebook, Instagram, TikTok)
- Hospital information desk and flyers

4. Customer Relationships
- Personalized nutrition suggestions based on health status
- Self-service through app or website
- Chat support for orders and dietary questions
- Loyalty programs (points for each order)
- Subscription meal plans for staff, patients, and external users
- Feedback system for ongoing improvement

5. Revenue Streams
- Direct food sales (in-restaurant and online)

- Delivery charges (inside the hospital and outside)

- Subscription meal plans (daily, weekly, or monthly)
- Diet-specialized meal packages
- Corporate and staff meal deals
- Partnerships with hospitals and nutrition departments
- Catering services for hospital events
- Healthy snacks and packaged foods

6. Key Activities
- Preparing healthy, recovery-oriented meals
- Running the e-commerce platform (website and app)
- Delivering orders inside and outside the hospital
- Menu development with nutritionists
- Customer support and order management
- Procuring fresh ingredients
- Marketing and social media promotion
- Managing subscriptions and meal plans

7. Key Resources
- Kitchen and restaurant space within the hospital
- Qualified chefs
- Nutritionists and dieticians
- Delivery team (runners inside the hospital and riders for outside)
- E-commerce system (website, app, and database)
- Logistics technology (order tracking and point-of-sale system)
- Supplier relationships (farmers and distributors)
- Branding and marketing materials

8. Key Partners

- Hospitals (for space, access, and patient dietary coordination)
- Nutritionists and medical dieticians
- Food suppliers, local farmers, and beverage distributors
- Delivery partners or own delivery fleet
- App developers and IT service providers
- Payment service providers (mobile money, cash, bank transfer, etc.)
- Nearby pharmacies or wellness centers for cross-promotion.

- Health insurance providers
- NGOs and institutional partners supporting health and nutrition(RBC,MoH,Gvt,etc)

9. Cost Structure
- Staff salaries (chefs, delivery personnel, IT, cleaners, etc.)
- Kitchen and restaurant equipment
- Food supplies and raw ingredients
- Technology development and maintenance (app and website)
- Marketing and advertising
- Delivery operations (packaging)
- Rent or commission to hospital for operating space
- Utilities (water, electricity, internet)
- Packaging and hygiene supplies
